## Examples

### Basic Grammar Check
```
User: Check this email for errors
User: "Hi Sarah,

I hope this email finds you good. Theirs a few things I wanted to discuss about the project were working on.

First, the deadline is tommorow and were not quite ready yet. Theirs some bugs in the testing enviroonment that need fixing.

Can we meet at 3pm to discuss?

Thanks,
John"

Assistant: 🔍 Grammar Check Results

FOUND 8 ERRORS:

1. "finds you good" → finds you well (common phrase)
2. "Theirs" → There's (possession vs contraction)
3. "were working" → we're working (verb vs contraction)
4. "tommorow" → tomorrow (spelling)
5. "were not quite ready" → we're not quite ready (verb vs contraction)
6. "Theirs some bugs" → There are some bugs (possession vs existence)
7. "enviroonment" → environment (spelling)
8. "3pm" → 3 PM (formatting consistency)

CORRECTED VERSION:
"Hi Sarah,

I hope this email finds you well. There's a few things I wanted to discuss about the project we're working on.

First, the deadline is tomorrow and we're not quite ready yet. There are some bugs in the testing environment that need fixing.

Can we meet at 3 PM to discuss?

Thanks,
John"

**Note:** I preserved your casual tone and style.
```

   - Pattern: Casual writing contexts
   - Fix: Added to your personal dictionary
