# Draft Email

Generate professional, effective emails for any communication scenario with appropriate tone, structure, and clarity.

## What This Skill Does

The Draft Email skill helps you:
- Draft professional emails for any scenario
- Adjust tone based on recipient and context
- Structure emails for maximum effectiveness
- Include all necessary information clearly
- Follow email etiquette and best practices
- Save time while maintaining quality

## Usage

**Basic Usage (L1):**
```
/draft-email [recipient] [topic] --purpose [goal]
/draft-email reply --context [previous conversation]
/draft-email template [type] --customize [details]
```

**Advanced Usage (L2):**
```
/draft-email --tone [formal/casual/friendly] --audience [executive/peer/client]
/draft-email --from-reference [similar email] --adapt [new context]
/draft-email polish --existing-draft [text] --improve [aspects]
```

**Expert Usage (L3):**
```
/draft-email --learn-from [sent emails] --style-match
/draft-email --sequence [multi-email campaign] --maintain-context
/draft-email --negotiation --strategy [collaborative/competitive]
```

### Best Practices

1. **Keep subject lines clear and specific** - Recipient should know what the email is about before opening
2. **Get to the point quickly** - State purpose in first paragraph
3. **Be specific** - Include dates, times, numbers, and concrete details
4. **Have clear call to action** - Tell recipient what you want them to do
5. **Proofread** - Check for typos, clarity, and tone
6. **Keep it concise** - Most emails should be under 200 words
7. **Consider the recipient** - Adjust tone and formality based on relationship

### Common Mistakes

❌ **Mistake 1:** Vague subject line like "Question"
- Fix: Use specific subject like "Meeting Request: Q1 Report Review"

❌ **Mistake 2:** Burying the request in last paragraph
- Fix: State purpose clearly in first paragraph

❌ **Mistake 3:** Too long, rambling emails
- Fix: Keep concise, break into bullet points if needed

❌ **Mistake 4:** No clear call to action
- Fix: End with specific next step or question

❌ **Mistake 5:** Wrong tone for relationship
- Fix: Match formality to your relationship with recipient

- Make more diplomatic (soften criticism, find common ground)

**Email Polishing:**

- Adjust tone for recipient
- Strengthen subject line
- Optimize call to action

### Advanced Best Practices

3. **Adjust length based on recipient** - Executives want concise, colleagues may want detail
4. **Use diplomacy for difficult topics** - Frame feedback constructively, find common ground
5. **Polish before sending** - Always review for clarity, tone, and completeness
6. **Learn from patterns** - Notice which emails get good responses, replicate the style

### Advanced Common Mistakes

❌ **Mistake 1:** One tone for all recipients
- Fix: Adjust based on audience and relationship

❌ **Mistake 2:** Too formal for internal team, too casual for executives
- Fix: Match formality to context and relationship level

❌ **Mistake 3:** No consideration of cultural differences
- Fix: Research cultural norms for international recipients

❌ **Mistake 4:** Sending drafts without polishing
- Fix: Always review for clarity, flow, and tone
