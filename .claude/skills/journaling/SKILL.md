# Journaling

Daily journaling with guided prompts, reflection, gratitude tracking, and mood analysis.

## When to Use This Skill

Use when you want to:
- Start a daily journaling habit
- Reflect on your day with structure
- Track gratitude and positive moments
- Monitor your mood over time
- Gain self-awareness and insights

## How It Works

### Level 1: Daily Journaling (Sessions 3-10)

I'll help you:
- Complete a daily journal entry (5-10 minutes)
- Answer 3-5 guided prompts
- Track gratitude (3 things daily)
- Log your mood
- Review previous entries

**Example:**
```
User: Start my journal entry for today
User: I'm grateful for: my team, coffee, sunshine
User: My mood today: productive but tired
User: One thing I learned: prioritizing work matters
User: Show me my journal from last week
```

### Level 2: Reflection & Patterns (Sessions 11-25)

I'll also help you:
- Identify mood patterns over time
- Analyze what makes good vs. bad days
- Weekly reflection summaries
- Track recurring themes
- Suggest areas to focus on

**Example:**
```
User: What's my mood trend this month?
User: What makes my good days good?
User: What patterns do you see in my journal?
User: Generate a weekly reflection summary
User: What should I focus on this month?
```

### Level 3: Deep Self-Awareness (Sessions 26+)

I'll also help you:
- Identify blind spots and triggers
- Track personal growth over months
- Goal alignment with journal reflections
- Emotional intelligence insights
- Long-term trend analysis

**Example:**
```
User: What are my blind spots?
User: How have I grown in the last 3 months?
User: Do my actions align with my goals?
User: What triggers my stress?
User: Analyze my emotional patterns this year
```

## Daily Journal Template

### Level 1 (5-10 minutes)

1. **Gratitude** (3 things you're grateful for today)
2. **Highlight** (one thing that went well)
3. **Challenge** (one thing that was hard)
4. **Learning** (one thing you learned)
5. **Mood** (how you're feeling today)
6. **Tomorrow** (one thing to look forward to)

### Level 2 (adds reflection)

7. **Pattern** (any recurring themes today?)
8. **Energy** (what drained / energized you?)
9. **Alignment** (did your actions match your intentions?)

### Level 3 (adds depth)

10. **Growth** (how did you grow today?)
11. **Insight** (any new self-awareness?)
12. **Triggers** (any emotional triggers noticed?)
13. **Values** (which values did you live today?)

## Best Practices

1. **Consistency > Perfection:** Even 2 minutes is better than skipping
2. **Honesty helps:** Be truthful in your journal
3. **Review periodically:** Look back weekly or monthly
4. **Focus on growth:** What did you learn, not just what happened
5. **Protect your journal:** This is personal reflection time

## Tips for Success

1. **Start simple:** 3 prompts (gratitude, highlight, challenge)
2. **Same time daily:** Makes it a habit (morning or evening)
3. **Be honest:** Don't edit for others - this is for you
4. **Review weekly:** Look back to see patterns and growth
5. **Don't judge:** Some days are hard - that's okay

## Common Mistakes

- Skipping days (makes pattern analysis harder)
- Being too brief (you need enough detail for insights)
- Judging yourself (journaling is for reflection, not perfection)
- Never reviewing (you miss the growth insights)
- Writing for others (this is personal, be honest)
