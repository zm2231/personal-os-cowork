# Territory Planning

Design and optimize sales territories, assign quotas, and maximize territory productivity through data-driven analysis.

## Progressive Disclosure Tiers

### Level 1: Foundation

#### Core Capabilities
- **Territory Design**: Define territories based on geography, industry, or customer segment
- **Quota Setting**: Set quotas based on territory potential, historical performance, and company objectives
- **Account Segmentation**: Segment accounts by value (A, B, C) for prioritization
- **Territory Balance**: Ensure territories are balanced in terms of opportunity and workload
- **Rep Assignment**: Assign reps to territories based on experience, skills, and relationships

#### Workflows

**Territory Design Workflow:**
1. Analyze market and customer data (company size, industry, location)
2. Define territory criteria (geography, industry, segment)
3. Group accounts into territories
4. Assess territory potential (total addressable market, existing pipeline)
5. Balance territories (similar potential and workload)
6. Assign reps to territories

**Quota Setting Workflow:**
1. Assess territory potential (TAM, pipeline, growth rate)
2. Review historical performance (prior year bookings, win rate)
3. Set territory quota aligned with company objectives
4. Adjust for rep experience and tenure
5. Communicate quota with rationale
6. Monitor and adjust quarterly based on performance

#### Best Practices
- Use data to design territories, not arbitrary decisions
- Ensure territories are balanced in potential and workload
- Set challenging but achievable quotas (stretch but not impossible)
- Consider rep skills and experience in territory assignment
- Review and adjust territories annually or as needed

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Creating too many or too few territories (inefficient)
- Setting quotas based solely on last year's performance (no growth)
- Not balancing territories (some overloaded, some underutilized)
- Ignoring rep experience and relationships
- Not adjusting territories based on market changes

### Level 2: Advanced

#### Core Capabilities
- **Data-Driven Territory Optimization**: Use analytics to optimize territory boundaries and assignments
- **Account-Based Territory Planning**: Design territories around strategic accounts, not just geography
- **Quota Modeling**: Use historical data to model quota attainment and adjust for factors
- **Territory Performance Analysis**: Analyze territory performance, identify top and bottom performers
- **Territory Realignment**: Realign territories based on performance, market changes, or organizational changes

#### Workflows

**Territory Optimization Workflow:**
1. Collect data (account value, potential, existing relationships, pipeline)
2. Analyze territory performance (bookings, win rate, pipeline velocity)
3. Identify underperforming and overperforming territories
4. Test territory boundary changes (simulation)
5. Optimize territory assignments (rep skills, relationships, workload)
6. Implement changes with communication and transition plans

**Account-Based Territory Planning Workflow:**
1. Identify strategic accounts based on revenue potential and strategic importance
2. Map account locations and relationships
3. Design territories around strategic accounts
4. Assign rep teams to strategic accounts (AE, SDR, CSM)
5. Create account-based playbooks and resources
6. Monitor strategic account penetration and expansion

#### Best Practices
- Use analytics and simulation to optimize territories
- Design territories around strategic accounts, not just geography
- Test territory changes before implementation (simulate)
- Consider rep relationships and strengths in assignment
- Monitor territory performance and adjust proactively

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Optimizing for one metric without considering others (e.g., revenue only, not workload)
- Not testing territory changes before implementation
- Ignoring rep relationships and account history
- Realigning territories too frequently (disruption)
- Not communicating territory changes clearly to reps

---

### Level 3: Strategic

#### Core Capabilities
- **AI-Powered Territory Optimization**: Use machine learning to optimize territory boundaries and assignments
- **Dynamic Territory Management**: Implement dynamic territory management that adjusts based on performance and market conditions
- **Territory Operations Infrastructure**: Build scalable territory management infrastructure and automation
- **Strategic Territory Planning**: Align territories with strategic objectives (market expansion, new segments, acquisitions)
- **Cross-Functional Territory Alignment**: Align territories with marketing campaigns, customer success regions, and partner territories

#### Workflows

**AI-Powered Territory Optimization Workflow:**
1. Collect comprehensive data (accounts, potential, relationships, rep skills, performance)
2. Train ML model to optimize territory boundaries and assignments
3. Run simulations for different scenarios (rep count, market changes)
4. Generate optimized territory design recommendations
5. Implement changes with transition plans and communication
6. Monitor results and refine model quarterly

**Strategic Territory Planning Workflow:**
1. Define strategic objectives (new market entry, segment expansion, geographic expansion)
2. Design territories aligned with strategic objectives
3. Set territory quotas aligned with strategic targets
4. Allocate resources (reps, marketing, customer success) to strategic territories
5. Monitor territory progress toward strategic objectives
6. Adjust territories and resources as needed

#### Best Practices
- Use AI to augment, not replace, human judgment
- Design territories aligned with long-term strategy, not short-term revenue
- Build scalable infrastructure and automation for territory management
- Align territories across marketing, sales, and customer success
- Monitor and adjust territories based on market conditions

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Relying solely on AI without human oversight
- Designing territories for short-term revenue vs. long-term strategy
- Not aligning territories across functions (marketing, sales, customer success)
- Building manual territory processes that don't scale
- Not adjusting territories based on market changes

## Examples

### Example 1: Territory Design

**Level 1 Application:**
- **Market**: Northeast US, 500 accounts
- **Design**: 3 territories (NY/NJ, New England, Mid-Atlantic)
- **Balance**: Each territory ~$2M potential, 160-170 accounts
- **Assignments**: Senior rep to NY/NJ (largest), 2 reps to other territories
- **Result**: Balanced territories, each rep has similar opportunity

### Example 2: Quota Modeling

**Level 2 Application:**
- **Territory**: Southeast US, $3M potential, existing pipeline $1M
- **History**: Prior year $1.2M bookings, 40% win rate
- **Modeling**: Target $2M (growth of $800k), achievable with 45% win rate on pipeline
- **Quota**: Set at $2M (67% of potential, stretch goal)
- **Rep**: 2nd year rep, achieved 90% of quota last year
- **Adjustment**: Quota reduced to $1.8M based on rep experience
- **Result**: Rep achieved $1.85M (103% of adjusted quota)

### Example 3: Account-Based Territory Planning

**Level 2 Application:**
- **Strategic Accounts**: 20 enterprise accounts with $10M+ potential
- **Design**: Dedicated enterprise team (3 AEs, 2 SDRs, 1 CSM)
- **Territories**: Each AE manages 6-7 strategic accounts
- **Resources**: Playbooks, competitive intel, executive engagement
- **Result**: Strategic account penetration 30% in year 1, targeted 50% in year 2

### Example 4: AI-Powered Territory Optimization

**Level 3 Application:**
- **Data**: 1,000 accounts, rep skills (industry expertise, relationships), performance data
- **Model**: ML-optimized territory assignments to maximize revenue
- **Recommendations**: Reassign 15 accounts to different reps, create 2 new territories
- **Simulation**: Model predicts 15% revenue increase with optimized assignments
- **Implementation**: Gradual transition over 3 months with communication
- **Result**: Revenue increased 12% in first 6 months after optimization
