# Tone Adjuster

Fine-tune the emotional tone and register of your writing—confident, empathetic, urgent, diplomatic—while preserving the core message.

## When to Use This Skill

Use when you want to:
- Sound more confident in your writing
- Show empathy in difficult messages
- Create urgency without panic
- Be diplomatic in sensitive situations
- Match the emotional needs of your audience

## Tone Adjustments I Support

### Level 1: Basic Tones

**Confident ↔ Hesitant:**
- Add/remove qualifying words (maybe, possibly)
- Use definitive vs. tentative language
- Adjust certainty in statements
- Change from "I think" to "I know"
- Add/remove hedging phrases

**Empathetic ↔ Direct:**
- Add/remove emotional acknowledgment
- Include/exclude "I understand" phrases
- Adjust concern level
- Change perspective-taking
- Add/remove supportive language

**Urgent ↔ Relaxed:**
- Add/remove time pressure words
- Change pacing and rhythm
- Add/remove action language
- Adjust deadline framing
- Use active vs. passive urgency

**Diplomatic ↔ Blunt:**
- Soften or strengthen directness
- Add/remove qualifiers
- Adjust conflict level
- Change how criticism is framed
- Use polite vs. straightforward language

### Level 2: Nuanced Combinations

**Confident + Humble:**
- State expertise clearly
- Acknowledge limitations
- Show confidence without arrogance
- Invite collaboration
- Balance assertion with openness

**Urgent + Calm:**
- Convey importance
- Avoid panic language
- Focus on action, not stress
- Keep composure
- Use measured urgency

**Empathetic + Professional:**
- Show understanding
- Maintain boundaries
- Address emotions appropriately
- Keep professional tone
- Balance warmth with business

**Direct + Kind:**
- Be clear and straightforward
- Use kind language
- Avoid harshness
- Show respect
- Maintain honesty

### Level 3: Advanced Adaptations

**Emotional Intelligence:**
- Read audience emotional state
- Match emotional tone
- De-escalate tense situations
- Build trust through tone
- Navigate sensitive topics

**Multi-Tone Generation:**
- Different tones for different recipients
- Context-aware tone selection
- Audience segmentation by tone preference
- Tone A/B testing
- Personalized tone delivery

**Tone Coaching:**
- Identify tone issues in your writing
- Suggest tone improvements
- Teach tone principles
- Practice tone adjustment
- Track tone growth over time

## Best Practices

1. **Match the moment:** Tone should fit the situation
2. **Authenticity first:** Don't fake emotions
3. **Audience matters:** Their emotional state guides your tone
4. **Balance extremes:** Rarely is one extreme right
5. **Get feedback:** Learn how tone is received

## Examples

### Hesitant to Confident
```
User: Make this more confident

User: "I think this might be a good approach for the project. Possibly we could try it. I'm not sure if it's the best solution, but maybe it could work."

Assistant: 💪 Confident Version

"This approach is the right solution for our project. We should implement it immediately. It will achieve our goals effectively and deliver the results we need."

**Tone changes:**
- "I think this might be" → "This is" (definitive)
- "possibly we could try" → "We should implement" (action)
- "I'm not sure if" → [removed] (no hedging)
- "maybe it could work" → "It will... effectively" (certainty)

**Confidence indicators added:**
- Definite language (is, will)
- Action-oriented (implement, achieve)
- Outcome-focused (deliver results)
- No qualifiers

Core meaning preserved, confidence increased significantly.
```

### Adding Empathy to Rejection
```
User: Add empathy to this rejection

User: "Your application was not approved. You don't meet the qualifications. Good luck elsewhere."

Assistant: 💗 Empathetic Version

"Thank you so much for your application and for the time you put into it.

I know this news is disappointing, and I want to acknowledge your effort and interest in joining us. After careful consideration, we've decided to move forward with candidates whose experience more closely matches the specific needs of this role.

This decision doesn't diminish your qualifications or potential. You clearly have valuable skills, and I encourage you to continue pursuing opportunities that align with your strengths.

I'd be happy to provide feedback if you'd find it helpful.

Wishing you the best in your next steps."

**Empathy elements added:**
- Acknowledged effort ("Thank you for your time")
- Validated feelings ("I know this news is disappointing")
- Softened rejection ("decision doesn't diminish your qualifications")
- Encouraged future success ("encourage you to continue")
- Offered support ("happy to provide feedback")

**Changes:**
- "Not approved" → "decided to move forward" (softer)
- "Don't meet qualifications" → "experience more closely matches" (less blunt)
- Added supportive and encouraging language
- Maintained clear message while being human
```

### Urgent but Calm
```
User: Make this urgent but not panicked

User: "The server is down! Everything is broken! We need to fix this right now! This is a disaster!"

Assistant: ⚡ Urgent but Calm Version

"Server is currently down. We need to address this immediately.

Status: All services offline
Impact: Users unable to access platform
Priority: Critical

Action items:
1. Investigate root cause
2. Initiate recovery procedures
3. Estimate recovery time
4. Communicate with stakeholders

Let's handle this systematically and efficiently. Updates to follow."

**Urgency maintained:**
- "immediately" (time-critical)
- "Critical" (priority level)
- Action items listed clearly

**Panic removed:**
- No exclamation marks
- No emotional language ("disaster", "everything is broken")
- Focused on facts and actions
- "systematically and efficiently" (not panicked)

**Result:** Clear urgency without stress. Team knows it's important but stays focused.
```

### Diplomatic Feedback
```
User: Make this diplomatic

User: "Your work is sloppy and you're always late. You need to fix this immediately or we'll have problems."

Assistant: 🤝 Diplomatic Version

"I'd like to discuss some areas where we can improve together.

I've noticed that recent work has had some quality issues and deadlines have been missed a few times. I know you're capable of strong performance, so I want to help you get back on track.

Let's identify what's causing these challenges and create a plan to address them. I'm confident we can get this resolved with some focused effort.

Would you like to schedule a time to talk this through?"

**Diplomatic elements:**
- "discuss some areas" (vs. "sloppy work")
- "I've noticed" (vs. accusation)
- "capable of strong performance" (acknowledges strengths)
- "help you get back on track" (supportive)
- "let's identify... together" (collaborative)

**Changes:**
- Removed personal attacks ("sloppy", "always late")
- Removed threats ("or we'll have problems")
- Changed from "you" problems to "we can improve"
- Invited conversation rather than demanding
- Maintained core message: quality and timeliness need improvement
```

### Multi-Tone Generation (Level 2)
```
User: Generate this announcement in 3 tones

User: "We're changing our pricing next month"

Assistant: 🎨 Three Tone Versions

**1. Confident & Professional:**
"We're implementing a new pricing structure next month.

This change reflects the enhanced value we're delivering and ensures we can continue investing in product improvements.

Your current rate remains locked in until December 2026. You'll receive a detailed breakdown of changes by next Friday.

We're excited about this next phase of growth."

**2. Empathetic & Transparent:**
"I want to share some news about upcoming changes to our pricing.

I know pricing changes can be concerning, so I want to be transparent about why we're making this decision. We're adding significant new features and support that will benefit all our customers.

Your current rate is protected until December 2026, giving you time to adjust. I'll personally walk through the details with anyone who wants more information.

Your feedback has been invaluable in shaping these changes."

**3. Urgent & Action-Oriented:**
"Important: Pricing structure changes effective next month.

New pricing reflects substantial feature enhancements and service upgrades.

Your existing rate is grandfathered until December 2026. Review the changes arriving in your inbox by Friday.

Contact your account representative with immediate questions."

**Quick comparison:**
- Confident: Professional, value-focused, forward-looking
- Empathetic: Caring, transparent, supportive
- Urgent: Direct, action-focused, time-bound

Which tone matches your need?
```

### Emotional Intelligence Coaching (Level 3)
```
User: Why does this email sound off?

User: [Draft email to client about delay]

Assistant: 🧠 Emotional Intelligence Analysis

CURRENT DRAFT:
"Hey, just wanted to let you know we're delayed on the project. Stuff came up and we're running behind. Sorry about that. We'll get it to you when we can."

**ISSUES:**

1. **Dismissive language:** "Just wanted to let you know" - downplays importance
   → Client thinks you don't care about their project

2. **Vague explanation:** "Stuff came up" - sounds unprofessional
   → Client wonders what's really happening

3. **Weak commitment:** "When we can" - no timeline
   → Client feels uncertain and unimportant

4. **Casual apology:** "Sorry about that" - insufficient
   → Client doesn't feel heard or respected

**MISSING:**
- Acknowledgment of impact on client
- Specific timeline (even if estimated)
- Assurance of quality despite delay
- Professional accountability

**SUGGESTED REWRITE (High EQ):**

"Hi [Client Name],

I need to let you know about a schedule adjustment for your project.

We've encountered an unexpected challenge that requires additional time to resolve properly. I want to ensure we deliver the quality work you expect, rather than rushing a solution that doesn't meet our standards.

**New timeline:** [Specific date]
**Impact on you:** Minimal, as [explain what's affected]
**What we're doing:** [Specific action to resolve]

I sincerely apologize for this delay and for any inconvenience it causes. Your project is a priority, and I'm personally overseeing the resolution.

I'll update you daily until completion. Thank you for your understanding."

**EQ Elements:**
- Professional urgency (not casual)
- Specific details (builds trust)
- Acknowledges client impact (shows empathy)
- Takes responsibility (accountability)
- Commits to communication (reassurance)

Want me to apply this rewrite?
```

## Tips for Success

1. **Know your audience:** Their emotional state matters
2. **Be authentic:** Don't fake emotions
3. **Balance extremes:** Rarely is extreme tone appropriate
4. **Context is king:** Situation guides the right tone
5. **Learn and adjust:** See how tone is received

## Common Mistakes

- Overcompensating (going from too hesitant to too arrogant)
- Adding fake emotions that don't feel genuine
- One extreme to another (missing middle ground)
- Ignoring audience emotional state
- Not learning from tone feedback
