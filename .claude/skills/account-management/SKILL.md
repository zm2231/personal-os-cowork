# Account Management

Manage customer relationships post-sale, ensure success, and drive expansion through upselling and retention.

## Progressive Disclosure Tiers

### Level 1: Foundation (Available Session 24+)

#### Core Capabilities
- **Onboarding Management**: Guide customers through implementation, ensure adoption milestones achieved, reduce time-to-value
- **Success Planning**: Create customer success plans with objectives, milestones, and success metrics
- **Relationship Building**: Develop trusted advisor relationships, understand business goals, communicate value regularly
- **Issue Resolution**: Proactively identify and resolve issues, coordinate with support, prevent churn
- **Health Monitoring**: Track customer health (usage, satisfaction, engagement), flag at-risk accounts

#### Workflows

**Customer Onboarding Workflow:**
1. Kickoff meeting to understand objectives and success criteria
2. Implementation plan with milestones and timeline
3. Regular check-ins to track progress and resolve blockers
4. Training sessions for end users
5. Go-live support and optimization
6. Value realization review to confirm ROI

**Customer Health Monitoring Workflow:**
1. Define health metrics (usage, NPS, adoption, satisfaction)
2. Monitor metrics weekly/monthly
3. Flag accounts with declining health (usage down, NPS drop, key contact changes)
4. Create intervention plans for at-risk accounts
5. Communicate health status internally to sales, product, and leadership

#### Best Practices
- Be proactive, not reactive (address issues before customers report them)
- Understand customer business goals, not just product usage
- Regularly communicate value and ROI (don't assume they know)
- Build relationships with multiple stakeholders (champion, budget owner, end users)
- Document all interactions in the CRM

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Over-promising during onboarding (set realistic expectations)
- Not following up after implementation (set and forget)
- Ignoring at-risk signals (usage decline, NPS drop, complaints)
- Only engaging when renewal is due (relationship decay)
- Not understanding the full customer organization

### Level 2: Advanced (Available Session 24+)

#### Core Capabilities
- **Strategic Account Planning**: Develop multi-year account plans, identify expansion opportunities, align with customer strategy
- **QBR Execution**: Conduct Quarterly Business Reviews, present value delivered, discuss future roadmap
- **Cross-Functional Coordination**: Coordinate sales, support, product, and success teams for account success
- **Expansion Opportunity Identification**: Identify upsell/cross-sell opportunities, time expansions strategically, leverage success stories
- **Churn Reduction**: Analyze churn risk factors, implement retention programs, win back churned customers

#### Workflows

**QBR Preparation and Execution Workflow:**
1. Collect data (usage metrics, ROI achieved, milestones completed)
2. Prepare presentation (value delivered, success stories, recommendations)
3. Schedule meeting with stakeholders (champion, budget owner, executives)
4. Present value and ROI achieved to date
5. Discuss future roadmap and strategic alignment
6. Identify expansion opportunities and next steps

**Expansion Opportunity Identification Workflow:**
1. Analyze customer usage and identify gaps (unused features, under-utilized modules)
2. Understand customer roadmap and strategic initiatives
3. Map expansion opportunities to customer goals
4. Time expansions strategically (after success demonstrated, before budget cycles)
5. Coordinate with sales team for pricing and proposal

#### Best Practices
- Prepare thoroughly for QBRs (data-driven, value-focused)
- Involve customer executives in strategic discussions
- Time expansions after delivering clear value (don't expand too early)
- Use customer success stories to influence expansion
- Measure and track NRR (Net Revenue Retention) as key metric

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Making QBRs product-focused instead of business-value focused
- Not understanding customer strategic objectives
- Expanding too early before delivering value (trust erosion)
- Not involving sales team in expansion process
- Ignoring churn risk until it's too late

### Level 3: Strategic (Available Session 24+)

#### Core Capabilities
- **Executive Relationship Management**: Build C-level relationships, influence customer strategy, become strategic partner
- **Customer Advisory Board**: Engage top customers in product development, create advocate community, drive product roadmap
- **Customer Lifecycle Management**: Optimize full customer lifecycle (onboarding → adoption → expansion → advocacy)
- **Strategic Partnership Development**: Develop joint go-to-market partnerships, co-develop solutions, share revenue
- **Customer Success Operations**: Build scalable success operations, playbooks, and metrics for team efficiency

#### Workflows

**Executive Relationship Management Workflow:**
1. Identify C-level stakeholders and their strategic priorities
2. Engage executives with business-value focused discussions (not product features)
3. Deliver executive briefings with industry insights and benchmarking
4. Become trusted advisor by providing continuous strategic value
5. Leverage executive relationships for strategic partnerships

**Customer Advisory Board Workflow:**
1. Identify top 10-20 customers with high NPS and expansion potential
2. Recruit advisory board members (executive sponsors, champions)
3. Host quarterly board meetings (virtual or in-person)
4. Discuss product roadmap, gather feedback, co-create solutions
5. Create advocate community for case studies and referrals

#### Best Practices
- Focus on business outcomes, not product features, in executive conversations
- Use customer feedback to influence product roadmap
- Create win-win partnerships (customer success = your success)
- Build scalable operations and playbooks for team efficiency
- Measure customer lifetime value (CLV) and optimize acquisition cost (CAC)

#### Common Mistakes to Avoid
- Not engaging executives early enough in customer lifecycle
- Using advisory board only for feedback without co-creation
- Designing partnerships that benefit only one party
- Scaling success operations without considering customer experience
- Focusing on short-term revenue vs. long-term customer value

## Examples

### Example 1: Customer Onboarding

**Level 1 Application:**
- **Customer**: New enterprise account, 500 users
- **Onboarding**: 8-week implementation plan, weekly check-ins, training sessions
- **Milestones**: Integration complete, training delivered, 80% user adoption
- **Result**: Time-to-value achieved in 8 weeks (vs. 12 weeks average), customer satisfied

### Example 2: QBR and Expansion

**Level 2 Application:**
- **Customer**: Mid-market account, $50k ARR
- **QBR**: Presented $200k savings achieved in first year, discussed expansion needs
- **Expansion**: Added new module to support growth, expanded to $75k ARR
- **Result**: 50% expansion, customer renewed for second year

### Example 3: Churn Reduction

**Level 2 Application:**
- **Situation**: Customer at risk (usage down 40%, key contact left, NPS dropped)
- **Intervention**: Executive call to understand concerns, dedicated success manager, risk remediation plan
- **Outcome**: Issues resolved, NPS improved from 30 to 70, customer renewed
- **Result**: Saved $50k ARR, customer became advocate

### Example 4: Strategic Partnership

**Level 3 Application:**
- **Customer**: Enterprise account, $500k ARR, strategic importance
- **Partnership**: Joint go-to-market program, co-developed industry solution, revenue sharing
- **Structure**: Customer gets exclusive solution, vendor gets co-marketing and upsell rights
- **Result**: Partnership generated $2M pipeline, expanded account to $1M ARR in 18 months
