# Presentation

Create compelling presentations and slide decks that tell a story, engage your audience, and drive action—from planning to delivery.

## When to Use This Skill

Use when you want to:
- Structure a presentation with clear narrative
- Create slide content and talking points
- Plan visual design and layout
- Practice and refine delivery
- Adapt presentations for different audiences

## How It Works

### Level 1: Presentation Planning (Sessions 12-20)

I'll help you:
- Define presentation goals and audience
- Structure content with compelling narrative
- Create slide outline and flow
- Develop key talking points
- Plan visual elements

**Example:**
```

User: "I need to present to the executive team next week"
```

### Level 2: Content & Visual Design (Sessions 21-40)

I'll also help you:

### Level 3: Advanced Presentation Strategies (Sessions 41+)

I'll also help you:
- Tailor presentations to different audiences
- Create interactive elements

- Practice delivery and timing

**Example:**

## Best Practices

1. **Start with audience:** Everything serves them
2. **One message per slide:** Simplicity wins
3. **Tell a story:** Humans remember narratives
4. **Data needs meaning:** Don't just show numbers
5. **Practice matters:** Rehearse out loud

### Q&A Preparation (Level 3)
```
User: Help me prepare for Q&A

User: "Presenting quarterly results to board. They're tough on financials. Last time they drilled me on margins."

Assistant: 🤔 Q&A Preparation Strategy

**LIKELY BOARD QUESTIONS (Based on past meetings):**

**Category 1: Financial Performance**

Q1: "Gross margins are down 2% from last quarter. Why?"
→ PREPARED ANSWER:
"Margins declined due to two factors:
1. One-time investment in quality assurance (+$X)
2. Product mix shift (higher volume, lower margin items)

Excluding these one-time items, margins are stable. Q2 pricing adjustments will recover this margin."
→ EVIDENCE: Show margin by product line

Q2: "Why did customer acquisition cost increase?"
→ PREPARED ANSWER:
"CAC increased 8% because we:
1. Expanded to new markets (higher acquisition cost)
2. Increased ad spend in competitive categories
3. Improved targeting quality (higher conversion)

Lifetime value increased 15%, so LTV/CAC ratio improved."
→ EVIDENCE: CAC vs. LTV trend chart

**Category 2: Strategic Decisions**

Q3: "Why did we prioritize Product A over Product B?"
→ PREPARED ANSWER:
"Product A has 3x larger TAM ($X vs. $Y)
Product A has shorter time-to-market (3 months vs. 6 months)
Customer demand is 4x higher for Product A

We're not deprioritizing Product B—it's scheduled for Q2."
→ EVIDENCE: Market analysis comparison

Q4: "What's the risk of this strategy not working?"
→ PREPARED ANSWER:
"Main risks and mitigations:
1. Market adoption slower than expected → Pivot marketing
2. Competitors respond → 2-year feature lead
3. Supply chain constraints → Diversified suppliers

We're de-risking through rapid iteration and customer feedback."
→ EVIDENCE: Risk matrix with mitigations

**Category 3: Team & Operations**

Q5: "Why is headcount growing faster than revenue?"
→ PREPARED ANSWER:
"Headcount growth is intentional:
- Engineering: Product backlog doubled, need capacity
- Sales: New markets require sales team
- Customer success: Retention is priority, not just acquisition

We expect revenue to catch up in Q2 as new products ship."
→ EVIDENCE: Headcount vs. revenue trajectory

**DIFFICULT QUESTIONS (If they push):**

Q: "Your projections seem optimistic. What if growth is half your target?"
→ REDIRECT STRATEGY:
"Even at 50% of target, we hit $X revenue, which meets baseline expectations. Our conservative case is 60% of target. We're planning for both scenarios."
→ HAVE READY: Conservative case projections

Q: "Have you considered [competitor's approach]?"
→ ACKNOWLEDGE & PIVOT:
"Yes, we've studied that approach. Our research shows customers prefer [your advantage]. However, we're monitoring their progress and will adapt if needed."
→ HAVE READY: Competitive analysis summary

**Q&A DELIVERY TIPS:**
1. Listen to full question before answering
2. Pause before responding (shows thoughtfulness)
3. Address the concern directly first
4. Support with data when possible
5. End confidently
6. If you don't know: "That's a great question. Let me research and get back to you."

**PRACTICE RECOMMENDATION:**
Role-play with me on 3-5 of these questions. Want to start?
```

- Too much text on slides
- Data without insights or meaning
- No clear narrative flow
- Skipping practice/rehearsal
- One presentation for all audiences
